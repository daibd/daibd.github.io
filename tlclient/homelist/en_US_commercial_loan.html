Shop: Regardless of second-hand shops or new shops,the applicant's repayment ability, rental return rate and duration of the leasewill be assessed. The loan amount of shops is generally 60%-70%, and some bankscan achieve 80%. The loan interest rate is slightly higher than the home loan.It mainly assesses the applicant's repayment ability, rental return rate andduration of the lease.

Franchise Business: Bank-specific approved Franchise Businessgenerally have a loan amount of 50% to 60% of the business value. If there is aproperty collateral, the amount can reach 80%, or as part of the mortgage,which will lower your loan interest rate.

Business loans: divided into real estate mortgages orunsecured loans. The unsecured loan amount is generally 50% of the businessvalue, and the mortgage loan amount can be higher.

Business loans require applicant information:

1. Past business experience and industry-related workexperience;

2. Two-year financial statements for the purchase ofthe business;

3. The repayment ability of the purchaser;

4. Whether the purchaser will work in this business,industry prospects, etc.;
